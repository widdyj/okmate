Welcome to the Netherlands! The bikes are great, the bread is questionable, and the tax system is a labyrinth designed by people who really like boxes. Let's walk through the parts that actually affect your wallet.

## The 30% ruling (the famous one)

If you were recruited from abroad and meet the salary threshold, the **30% ruling** lets your employer pay up to 30% of your gross salary tax-free as a reimbursement for "extraterritorial costs."

- It has been getting steadily less generous — the duration and percentage have been trimmed in recent reforms, so **check the current rules for your start date**
- It also historically let you opt for *partial non-resident status*, simplifying Box 2 and Box 3 — another benefit that's been phased down
- You generally have a limited window to apply after starting work. Don't sleep on it.

> The 30% ruling is the single biggest financial reason a lot of skilled migrants come out ahead in the NL. Confirm your eligibility before you sign anything.

## The three boxes

Dutch income tax is split into three "boxes":

| Box | What's in it | Vibe |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Box 1 | Salary, home ownership | The big one |
| Box 2 | Substantial shareholdings (5%+) | Founders & directors |
| Box 3 | Savings & investments | The controversial one |

## Box 3 is where it gets weird

Box 3 taxes your *assets*, not your actual returns — based on a presumed (fictional) yield. This has been the subject of major legal challenges and ongoing reform because, surprise, taxing people on returns they didn't earn is unpopular.

If you have meaningful savings or investments, this is the box to get advice on. The rules are genuinely in flux.

## Deductions worth knowing

- **Mortgage interest** (*hypotheekrenteaftrek*) is still deductible in Box 1, though it's been gradually capped
- **Study costs**, certain **healthcare costs**, and **charitable donations** can be deductible
- Keep every annual statement (*jaaropgave*) — you'll want them at filing time

## The practical advice

1. Apply for the 30% ruling immediately if eligible
2. Register with DigiD so you can actually file
3. If you have assets, pay an accountant — Box 3 alone justifies it
4. File on time; the Belastingdienst is patient about almost nothing

It's not as scary as it looks. It's a *different* kind of scary. You'll be fine.
