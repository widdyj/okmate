Every November the Dutch retail internet loses its mind. Bol turns orange-er than usual, Coolblue sends you 14 emails, and MediaMarkt insists everything is a *megakorting*. So I did the boring thing: I tracked prices across the big NL retailers for several weeks before and after Black Friday.

The results are exactly as cynical as you'd expect.

## The "deal" that isn't

A meaningful share of Black Friday "discounts" were anchored to a **higher reference price** that the product hadn't actually sold at for months. Classic move:

1. Price drifts down over autumn
2. Reference price quietly resets to the old high
3. Black Friday "discount" brings it back to... roughly the October price

> EU rules require showing the lowest price from the prior 30 days. Read that small print — it tells you whether the deal is real.

## Where the real deals were

Not everything is a trick. The genuine bargains clustered in a few categories:

- **Open-box and refurbished** at Coolblue/Bol — frequently the best true value
- **Last-gen flagship electronics** being cleared for new models
- **Big-ticket appliances** where even a modest % is real money
- **Own-brand items** (Coolblue's own accessories, etc.) with actual cuts

## How to not get played

1. **Track before you buy.** Use a price-history tool; if the "deal" matches the 30-day low, it's fine — if not, walk away.
2. **Know the real price.** Decide what *you'd* happily pay before you see the banner.
3. **Ignore the countdown timer.** Artificial urgency is the whole business model.
4. **Stack cashback.** Layer a cashback portal or card on top of a genuine discount.
5. **Check return windows.** Many retailers extend returns into January for holiday gifting — useful leverage.

## The verdict

Black Friday in the Netherlands is neither a scam nor a goldmine. It's a **noisy market where a few real deals hide among a lot of theater**. Bring data, bring patience, and buy the thing you were going to buy anyway — just cheaper.

Or don't buy anything and feel smug. That's also a valid points-and-money strategy.
