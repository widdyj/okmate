Let's be honest: the Albert Heijn Bonus card is the single most-owned loyalty card in the Netherlands, and roughly 90% of people use it as a "make the price not insulting" button. You scan it, the Bonus discounts apply, you go home. End of story.

That's leaving value on the table. Let me bore you with the details.

## Bonus is a dynamic discount, not a static one

The Bonus folder rotates weekly, but the real trick is the **personal Bonus** offers in the AH app. These are algorithmically targeted to what you already buy. The hack isn't to buy more — it's to *time* your purchases of staples around when they hit personal Bonus.

> Rule of thumb: never buy coffee, olive oil, or laundry detergent at full price. One of them is always on a deep Bonus somewhere in a 3-week window.

## Stack with Air Miles

A lot of people forgot Air Miles still exists. It does, and AH is a partner. You earn miles passively, and the redemption catalog occasionally has genuinely good value — especially when you convert to specific retail vouchers rather than the "experiences" that quietly devalue your miles.

- Link your Air Miles to your Bonus card once and forget it
- Check the redemption rate before converting — it fluctuates
- Avoid the low-value impulse redemptions near the checkout

## Koopzegels: the boomer hack that still works

The savings stamps (*spaarzegels* / Koopzegels) give you roughly a small percentage back, and crucially you get a tiny interest-like bonus when you fill a full book. It's not life-changing, but for a household that already shops at AH weekly, it's free money for sticking labels in a booklet.

## The actual strategy

1. Always scan the Bonus card (obviously)
2. Buy non-perishable staples only when they're on Bonus
3. Keep Air Miles linked and redeem for vouchers, not fluff
4. Run Koopzegels if you're a weekly AH household
5. Use a cashback credit card on top for one more layer

Do all five and you've turned a discount card into a modest, stackable little money machine. Your friends will love hearing about it. Promise.
