I've been customers of both American Express (Platinum) and Revolut for quite some years now and I often as ask myself the question whether I'm paying for what I need through their packages? At time of writing, I'm currently a customer of American Express Platinum, but I've just finished an extensive period of travel across multiple countries and won't be doing it again for while, so the time felt right to assess.

It's worth remembering before we dive into this that **these are two completely different types of cards.**
**American Express is a *credit card* and Revolut is a *debit card*.**

(If you don't know: A credit card spends the bank's money — you pay it back later. A debit card spends your own money directly from your account.)

To me, this was the single biggest factor that I needed to decide. Does my financial situation suit a credit card? Only you or a financial advisor can answer that question. For me: I think it does, but it ***doesn't need it***. But I had to remind myself that there are other credits out there, without the fancy benefits or price tag. That's a whole other article. I'm leaving it there before I go off on one.

## The breakdown
I side-by-side compared the benefits (I did this on 23 June 2026), which there is a lot of, to try and understand what I actually need. 

## Card Acceptance
It's true. American Express cards are not accepted everywhere in The Netherlands where payment by card is accepted, or a lot of other countries I've visited in fact. Sometimes I'm forced to pay with a Visa or Mastercard credit card. However this also goes for credit cards in general, some merchants only accept debit cards. Well - **Revolut uses Visa or Mastercard debit cards** and I've had significantly less rejections paying with it that I have my Amex.

Remember: Everytime you can't use it, you don't get your points. On to that...

**The winner for me: Revolut Ultra**

## Collecting and Redeeming Points
With both cards, expenditure per euro = points.
Americal Express Platinum payments collect Membership Reward points.
Revolut Ultra payments collect Rev Points.

Both can be exchanged to take money off a transaction you're making online, or redeemed to spend at variety of partners. I mostly use my points to exchange for partner airline loyalty scheme points to either purchase a last minute ticket or upgrade an existing flight. Again, this is a whole other article plenty of others have covered. Figure out what makes sense to you to spend them on.

| Benefit | Notes | Amex Membership Rewards | Rev Points |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Points per €** | Higher the better | 1 | 1 |
| **Exchange points** | **1 Flying Blue point=** | 2 | 1 |
| **Exchange points** | **1 British Airways avios=** | 1.25 | 1 |
| **Exchange points** | **1 Delta Sky Mile=** | 3 | 1 |
| **Exchange points** | **1 Cathay Pacific Asia Mile=** | 3 | 1 |

To understand the value here, let's say I want to take a direct flight to KIX (Osaka, Japan) with KLM which a quick look on [seats.aero](https://seats.aero) costs 30k Flying Blue points.

To get 30,000 points by just using my cards alone: **I need to spend €60,000 on my Amex Platinum vs €30,000 on my Revolut Ultra.**

### You can see Revolut is the winner here.

*HOWEVER... and it's a big caveat.*

American Express does a lot more points bonuses to get you there more faster. Infact I got 70.000 when I signed up and 60.000 when I introduced a friend. This rate varies a lot based on the time you sign up. I've never had that with Revolut.

Revolut does instead cash rewards for referrals and I've seen amounts between €10 and €200. So you're getting something by inviting friends to both.
