If you live in the Netherlands and you're not paying attention to **Flying Blue Promo Rewards**, you are leaving the easiest points value in Europe on the table. Schiphol is a KLM/Air France hub, which means award availability out of AMS is genuinely good — unlike basically everywhere else.

## What Promo Rewards actually are

Once a month, Flying Blue discounts a rotating list of routes by **25–50% off the normal mileage price**. The list changes on the 1st, the awards are valid for travel within a defined window, and the good ones disappear fast.

> A 25% off Promo Reward on a route you'd fly anyway is one of the highest-value redemptions in the entire program.

## How to win the monthly game

1. **Check on the 1st.** The new list drops at the start of the month. Set a calendar reminder.
2. **Search from AMS first.** Your home hub has the best availability. Don't overthink it.
3. **Be flexible on dates.** Promo award seats are capacity-controlled. Move your dates, not your destination.
4. **Watch the cash co-pay.** A "cheap" award with €180 in surcharges is not cheap. European short-haul usually has low fees; transatlantic on AF/KLM can sting.

## Earning the miles in the first place

You don't need to fly to rack up Flying Blue miles:

- **Amex Membership Rewards** transfers to Flying Blue 1:1 — this is the backbone of most NL points strategies
- **Bonus buys** during transfer bonuses (watch for 25–30% transfer promos)
- **Everyday spend** on a co-branded card if you fly KLM often enough to justify the fee

## The honest take

Flying Blue is not a "first class around the world for free" program. It's a *practical* program for people who actually live near a KLM hub and want cheap-ish flights to real destinations. Treat it like a discount engine, not a fantasy, and it pays off every single month.

Set the reminder. Check the 1st. Thank me later.
